4. RECIPE STANDARDS - SIGNATURE DISHES
================================================================================

NASI LEMAK SPECIAL (RM 12.00)
-----------------------------
Serving: 1 plate

RICE COMPONENT:
- Jasmine rice: 200g (cooked weight)
- Coconut milk: 100ml
- Pandan leaves: 2 pieces
- Salt: 1/2 tsp
- Method: Cook rice with coconut milk and pandan for fragrant texture

SAMBAL:
- Dried chilies: 50g (soaked, blended)
- Shallots: 30g
- Garlic: 10g
- Belacan: 5g
- Tamarind juice: 20ml
- Sugar: 15g
- Salt: to taste
- Method: Tumis until oil separates, 20-25 minutes

ACCOMPANIMENTS (per plate):
- Fried chicken: 1 piece (150g)
- Hard-boiled egg: 1/2 egg (sliced)
- Fried anchovies: 20g
- Roasted peanuts: 15g
- Cucumber slices: 3 pieces

PLATING: Rice in center, sambal on top, chicken to the side, egg and
anchovies arranged around rice, cucumber as garnish.

---

RENDANG TOK (RM 18.00)
----------------------
Serving: 1 portion (200g meat + gravy)

INGREDIENTS:
- Beef (topside): 200g cubed
- Coconut milk: 200ml
- Kerisik (toasted coconut): 50g
- Rempah rendang: 80g
- Lemongrass: 2 stalks (bruised)
- Galangal: 20g (sliced)
- Turmeric leaves: 2 pieces
- Kaffir lime leaves: 3 pieces
- Salt: to taste
- Sugar: 1 tsp

METHOD:
1. Toast coconut for kerisik until golden brown
2. Sauté rempah with oil until fragrant (10 min)
3. Add beef, coat well with rempah
4. Pour coconut milk, add lemongrass, galangal
5. Simmer on low heat for 3-4 hours
6. Add kerisik in last 30 minutes
7. Stir continuously until dry and caramelized
8. Add turmeric and lime leaves at the end

QUALITY CHECK: Meat should be tender, gravy thick and dark, oil separated.

---

AYAM PERCIK (RM 15.00)
----------------------
Serving: 1 portion (1/4 chicken)

MARINADE:
- Chicken: 300g (1/4 bird)
- Turmeric powder: 1 tsp
- Salt: 1/2 tsp
- Marinating time: Minimum 2 hours

PERCIK SAUCE:
- Coconut milk: 150ml
- Dried chilies: 30g (soaked, blended)
- Shallots: 50g
- Garlic: 20g
- Ginger: 15g
- Lemongrass: 1 stalk
- Galangal: 10g
- Turmeric: 1/2 tsp
- Sugar: 1 tbsp
- Salt: to taste

METHOD:
1. Marinate chicken with turmeric and salt
2. Grill chicken until 70% cooked
3. Prepare percik sauce - blend and cook until fragrant
4. Baste chicken generously with sauce
5. Continue grilling, basting every 2-3 minutes
6. Total grilling time: 25-30 minutes
7. Char marks should be visible

PLATING: Serve on banana leaf with extra sauce on the side.

5. COOKING METHODS & TECHNIQUES
================================================================================

TUMIS (Sautéing Malaysian Style)
--------------------------------
- Start with cold oil in hot wok
- Add aromatics (bawang, halia) first
- Add sambal/rempah, cook until oil separates
- "Pecah minyak" = oil separated = flavor developed
- Never rush this step - minimum 10-15 minutes

WOK HEY (Wok Breath)
--------------------
- Essential for: Char kuey teow, nasi goreng, mee goreng
- Wok must be smoking hot before adding ingredients
- Small batches only (max 1 portion at a time)
- Quick tossing action
- Total cooking time: 2-3 minutes max

GRILLING (Panggang)
-------------------
- Charcoal preferred for authentic flavor
- Maintain medium-high heat
- Baste frequently to prevent drying
- Internal temperature for chicken: 75°C
- Rest meat 2-3 minutes before serving

DEEP FRYING (Goreng)
--------------------
- Oil temperature: 170-180°C
- Never overcrowd the fryer
- Chicken pieces: 8-10 minutes depending on size
- Drain on rack, not paper towels
- Oil change: Every 3 days or when darkened

STEAMING (Kukus)
----------------
- Water must be boiling before placing food
- Don't lift lid during cooking
- Fish: 8-10 minutes per 500g
- Check doneness with fork/chopstick

7. FOOD STORAGE GUIDELINES
================================================================================

REFRIGERATOR ORGANIZATION (4°C or below):
-----------------------------------------
TOP SHELF:     Ready-to-eat foods, cooked items
MIDDLE SHELF:  Dairy, eggs, prepared ingredients
BOTTOM SHELF:  Raw meat and fish (in sealed containers)
DRAWERS:       Vegetables and fruits

FREEZER ORGANIZATION (-18°C or below):
--------------------------------------
- Raw proteins in sealed bags with date labels
- Maximum storage: Chicken 3 months, Beef 6 months, Fish 2 months
- Never refreeze thawed items

LABELING SYSTEM:
----------------
All items must have labels with:
- Item name
- Date prepared/received
- Use by date
- Staff initials

FIFO - FIRST IN, FIRST OUT:
---------------------------
Always use older stock first. New deliveries go to the back.

DRY STORAGE:
------------
- Temperature: Below 25°C, low humidity
- Off the floor (minimum 15cm)
- Away from walls (minimum 5cm)
- Check expiry dates weekly

10. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
================================================================================

FIRE:
-----
1. Shout "FIRE!" to alert others
2. Turn off gas at main valve
3. If small fire: Use fire extinguisher (PASS method)
   - Pull pin
   - Aim at base of fire
   - Squeeze handle
   - Sweep side to side
4. If large fire: Evacuate immediately
5. Call 999
6. Meet at assembly point (front parking lot)
7. Do not re-enter building

GAS LEAK:
---------
1. Do not operate any switches or create sparks
2. Turn off gas at main valve (if safe)
3. Open all windows and doors
4. Evacuate the area
5. Call gas emergency hotline
6. Do not re-enter until cleared

INJURY/ACCIDENT:
----------------
1. Administer first aid if trained
2. Call for supervisor
3. For serious injury: Call 999
4. Do not move injured person unless necessary
5. Complete incident report

POWER OUTAGE:
-------------
1. Remain calm
2. Turn off all electrical equipment
3. Check refrigerator/freezer - keep doors closed
4. If extended outage: Follow food safety protocol for temp control
5. Use backup lighting if available
